Abstract

In this paper,we propose a novel task of Manipulation QuestionAnswering(MQA),a class of Question Answering (QA) task,where the robot is required to ﬁnd the answer to the question by actively interacting with the environmentvia manipulation. Considering the tabletop scenario, a heatmap of the scene is generated to facilitate the robot to have a semantic understanding of the scene and an imitation learning approach with semantic understanding metric is proposed to generate manipulation actions which guide the manipulator to explore the tabletop to ﬁnd the answer to the question. Besides, a novel dataset which contains a variety of tabletop scenarios and corresponding question-answer pairs is established. Extensive experiments have been conducted to validate the effectiveness of the proposed framework.

Dataset

The objects in our dataset are divided into three Groups. Objects in Group I are often occluded by objects in Group II in this dataset. Other classes of objects are assigned to Group III. For each tabletop, we randomly select 20 objects and place them randomly on the tabletop to generate scene. Each scene is then adjusted to one of the three difficulty levels, easy, medium, and hard. For each scene, we randomly ask 20 questions. There are six kinds of questions, and we have solved three of them.

Algorithm

The proposed MQA model is composed of 3 modules, the semantic visual understanding module, manipulation planning module and question answering module. In the semantic visual understanding module, both the RGB and depth images are used to calculate a semantic heatmap. Then, the manipulation planning module is employed to generate a series of manipulation actions to guide the manipulator to explore the tabletop. Finally, with the initial image and the image obtained after the exploration, the question answering module outputs the answer to the question.

Experiment Results

Simulation experiment

We test our QA system in 600 new scenes. First, we use our action model to generate action. When the action model concludes that there is no need for action, the QA module will give an answer. The accuracy of the existence question is 0.80. The accuracy of the counting question is 0.52. The accuracy of the spatial question is 0.76. The accuracy of all questions is 0.68.

Real Experiments

We used UR5 robot, Kinect camera and real objects similar to dataset to build the experimental scene, and transferred our model from simulation to the real world directly.This is possible because the output of the model is a path independent of dynamics and the pictures in the simulation are similar to the actual pictures.

The robot first thinks there may be a key under the box, so the robot sucks it away and finds a key. After sucking the box away, there may be a key under the milk carton, so the robot pushes the milk carton away and finds no key. After two actions , the robot concludes there is no need for action and outputs the answer 1.
